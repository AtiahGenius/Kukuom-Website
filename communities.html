Our Communities

29 farming communities working together to build sustainable livelihoods.

Where We Work

The cooperative operates across 29 cocoa farming communities in the Asunafo South District of Ghana’s Ahafo Region. These communities form the backbone of our cooperative and contribute to sustainable cocoa production and rural development.

Members of Kukuom are organized into societies corresponding to their local communities. This structure allows for effective coordination, training, and distribution of resources.

Rural Economy & Livelihoods

The district is predominantly rural, with 83% of the population living in rural areas. Agriculture employs over 60 of the labor force, making it the primary source of livelihood for most households.

Agriculture in Our Communities

Cocoa is the dominant crop, contributing about 80% of agricultural activity. Farmers also cultivate oil palm, citrus, plantain, cassava, maize, rice, and vegetables to support food security and diversify incomes.

  • Cocoa farming as the primary income source.
  • Food crops for subsistence and local markets.
  • Livestock and aquaculture on a smaller scale.
Land Tenure System

Land ownership is vested in traditional authorities, with families granted usufruct rights to farm. Systems such as “Abunu” and “Abusa” allow farmers to cultivate land and share produce with landowners, promoting social balance and access to farmland.

Why Our Communities Matter

By organizing farmers across communities, the cooperative strengthens bargaining power, improves access to training and resources, and enables coordinated development projects that benefit entire villages.
