Women & Inclusion

Empowering women and promoting inclusive participation across our cooperative.

Our Commitment to Inclusion

Women play a vital role in cocoa production and community development. The cooperative is committed to increasing women’s participation in leadership, economic activities, and decision-making processes.

Kukuom Cooperative currently has 3,782 registered members, of which 1,512 are women, representing approximately 40 percent of total membership. Members are organized into 29 societies, each representing a farming community within the district.

Women Empowerment Programs

The cooperative supports initiatives that enhance women’s economic independence and leadership capacity.

  • Soap production and small business training.
  • Leadership and skills development programs.
  • Income diversification opportunities.
  • Support for women-led community initiatives.

Project Norah

Project Norah is a women-focused initiative that provides training and resources for soap production. This program helps women generate income, support their families, and contribute to local economic growth.

Our Goals for Inclusion
  • Increase women’s participation in leadership roles
  • Expand access to training and financial opportunities
  • Strengthen women-led enterprises
  • Promote gender equality across all cooperative activities
Why Inclusion Matters

Inclusive participation strengthens families, improves community resilience, and enhances sustainable development. By empowering women, the cooperative ensures broader economic growth and social stability.
