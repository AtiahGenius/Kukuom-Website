Governance & Leadership

Ensuring transparency, accountability, and effective support for our farmers.

Our Governance Structure

The cooperative is governed by an elected Board of Directors and supported by committees that oversee key areas such as premium management, child protection, environmental sustainability, and monitoring. This structure ensures accountability and effective decision-making.

Decision-making follows established cooperative principles and Fairtrade governance standards, with clear roles assigned to the Board of Directors, management team, and specialized committees.
The cooperative is governed by an elected Board of Directors consisting of 11 members, including three women.

The board is responsible for setting strategic direction, approving policies, and overseeing the performance of the cooperative. From the Board of Directors, an Executive Board of five members is selected to handle more frequent decision-making and operational oversight. The Executive Director is not a member of the Board but serves on the Executive Board in a management capacity.

Committees

To support effective governance and compliance, the cooperative operates through several sub-committees. These committees focus on specific areas and report to the Board of Directors.

  • The Premium Committee, who oversees the use and allocation of Fairtrade premiums.
  • The Child Labor Committee, who Leads awareness, prevention, and monitoring activities.
  • The Environment Committee,they are responsible for Promoting sustainable farming and environmental protection.
  • The Monitoring Committee, who Supports compliance, inspections, and internal controls.
Management and Staff

Day-to-day operations of the cooperative are managed by a small professional team. This includes an Executive Director, technical and administrative staff, and field trainers who work directly with farmers across the communities.
The cooperative currently employs nine staff members, including field trainers responsible for training farmers on good agricultural practices, environmental sustainability, and compliance with certification standards.
